Max Niebergall

Max Niebergall is a Student at Ursula Franklin Academy (UFA), in Toronto.

About Max Niebergall

Leadership

Since he was a child Max has attended summer camp at Camp Couchiching. After three years of leadership training at Couch, he was given the opportunity to become a councillor. The summer of 2015 will be Max's first year as a councillor, and his first paid position.

First Aid and Lifeguarding

In 2013 Max received the Bronze Start and Bronze Medallion certifications from the Life Saving Society of Canada, as well as a First Aid certification from the Canadian Red Cross in CPR C, and Standard First Aid. In 2014 he passed the qualifications and was certified as a Lifeguard in Canada by the Life Saving Society.

Technology and Computers

Max is a passionate about people and technology. He has competed in many National and School Board level computing competitions, including the Canadian Computing Competition (CCC), and ECCO.

Max has repeatedly excelled in computer related fields, and in 2014 he held a Tech Support position at Runnymede Jr. & Sr. Public School. The experience was coordinated by the CO-OP program at UFA, and Max received two credits for it.

Max has shown a passion for Computer Science throughout high school, joining the UFA Computer Competitions Team in his grade nine year. Max continued to participate in Programming Competitions and has taken every Computer Science course available at his school.

Max has become competent in Java, with aspects extending into Object Oriented Programming, and Graphical User Interfaces.

Max is also passionate about computer hardware. He has followed the hardware scene since he built his first computer with his dad and younger brother. Since then Max has gone on to build more than six desktop computers for himself and his friends. He is considered very knowledgeable by his peers.

Community Efforts

Max has been very involved in his school community. For several years he was on UFA's tech support team, which would support all aspects of tech in the school.

He has been an administrator for the school website, and has worked with school administration to migrate between website hosts.

He also headed up a team dedicated to providing knowledge to new students about how to safely and responsibly use the internet and computers in general. This E-Safety team covered best practices for passwords, social media, protecting against viruses and malware, and more.
